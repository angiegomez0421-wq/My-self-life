Featured Post 2: Critical Thinking & Media Literacy in the Supply Chain

In Logistics, information is currency. Critical Thinking helps us determine if a source is reliable. We shouldn't just believe the first transportation quote we receive!

Media Literacy is vital when reading logistics news:

  • Analyze the Source: Is the report about cost reduction published by an independent financial analyst or by the company itself?
  • Look for Bias: Headlines often use sensational language. A headline like, "Warehouse Disaster Causes Global Price Spike" might be hiding the fact that the company had already been facing mismanagement issues.
  • Cross-Reference Data: I apply this daily when comparing a carrier's promised delivery time (media/claim) with their actual performance data (reality).

Conclusion: We must use critical thinking to filter operational news and avoid making expensive decisions based on biased information.

Featured Post 3: Crime and Punishment: Securing Our Paper Assets

Cargo theft and internal fraud are serious threats to Dispapeles. Our products, like specialized paper rolls, are valuable, making us a target.

The Crime: The most common crime is pilferage (small thefts) or intentional damage to hide inventory errors.

Prevention: We use robust security cameras and a Trazability System. Every employee has to scan the product location and their ID.

Punishment: If an employee is caught committing fraud, they will be immediately terminated and legally prosecuted.

Lesson Learned: Strict controls and a clear understanding of legal consequences are essential for supply chain security.

Suggested Post 6: Green Logistics: A Current Affair

The global move toward Green Logistics is a major current affair. Dispapeles has to comply with environmental regulations. Our challenge is reducing the carbon footprint of our paper transportation.

If we had switched all our trucks to electric models, the cost of operations would have been much lower in the long run.

We must investigate more sustainable packaging options, like light wood or biodegradable plastic, even if they are currently more expensive.
